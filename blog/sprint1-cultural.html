sprint one - core: identity, values and strengths

23 April 2020

describe your personal values

1. Explain a situation where you have had an ethical decision to make. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made and the consequences of the action you took.

I am currently woking as a Personal Care Worker in an Aged Care Facility. In this position I have a Duty of Care within my scope of practice. There have been situations where I'm working in a pair with a co-worker and they either out of laziness or poor training, fail to do something that is quite important. Aged care recipiant has a right to dignity of living, and to be respected.

2. Describe how your culture has influenced your values and identity.

I have grown up in a largely religious culture which has instilled core conservative values. These have been shaped and changed through life experience, and through social interactions and cultural experiences abroad. I've had a close relationship with my grandparents who grew in rural country NZ/UK and so their hard work ethic has rubbed off onto me, as well as respect, and integrity. I have spent two years living in Auckland amongst friends who are Samoan/ Tongan and I really felt that the layers of my beliefs were aligned in terms of trying to understand the importance of language, culture, life, family, freedom and religion from a different perspective. In Melbourne I worked on a farm where I was the supervisor of 12 employees who were immigrants from Cambodia. Working with them for 2 and half years was incredibly rewarding and during the process of learning some of their language and working along-side them I gained a greater appreciation for communty and community support for them living in a foreign country. This led me to back-pack through SE-Asia in 2017 and delve-deeper into the hidden parts of the countries and cultures that I visited. This has led me to meet my wife Ingrid who is from Taiwan. Through this journey of self discovery I have found the values of inclusion, respect, and hardwork to be extremely valuable.

describe your strengths and limitations

1. Evaluate your strengths and limitations in terms of your learning and career development

Strengths: Passionate, extremely excited about learning, always seeking opportunities for opportunities to learn and progress. I am always thinking of how I can improve, what new skills will improve my careers prospects.

Limitations: If I'm stuck doing something that is mundane I struggle with motivation. Also if I have to do a lot of essay writing then I greatly lose interest. Really struggling with this assignment. I have a history of not finishing projects or things that I start. I try to be persistent, but working with a curriculum will definitely help me to stay focus on what is required in the moment.

2. Identify which of your strengths might help you in your learning journey and how they might intersect with learning obstacles.

I really enjoy learning by doing, and I am also a fan of learning by teaching others, this 'flipped classroom' mode of EDA really resonated with me as I researched what I wanted to pursue. I really enjoyed learning about neuroplasticity and undertanding this I think will help with focus to keep practicing.

3. Share an example from your experience of where you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss the strategies you used at that time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now, and why.

At work we were redisgning our production processes, after some push back, we decided to involve the entire production team to provide input in the new design. We spent the time explaining what we were trying to accomplish and they bought into it. We were able to redign with their input, and were able to implement them without many issues.
