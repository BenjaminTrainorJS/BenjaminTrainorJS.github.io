sprint two - core: learning reflections

22 April 2020

1. What is your long term goal and or career pathway?

From what I have seen with future growth and opportunities I think I would like to see how I go with DevOps. I believe to start with I should build a good strong foundation in the production life-cycle of software, and build experience into a Senior Dev role. I have been looking at Companies that I could see myself working for and definitely Atlassian is one of those companies that I could work for. They have Front-end and Java jobs that require a good grasp of React/JS and then Java as well. But I'm all for developing my language toolset.

2. What skills (non-technical - human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at EDA?

I come from a Operations background as senior supervior and as Production/Operations Manager. So I'd like to see how I can bring these sills to the table within the dev environment. Not necessarily managing people, but seeing how I can lead from a Developer perspective. How I can mentor, and support the growth of the the others in my cohort and project teams. Once I can gain the practical experience I think a role as a Senior Developer would suit me well as I enjoy mentoring and developing others.

3. A description of your own strengths and limitations

I'm bold, I like to make a decision and run with it. I'm passionate about people and how they can make or brake your organisation. A limitation is that sometimes I can struggle with Impostor syndrome, and that can rattle me a bit. Sometimes I'm not the greatet with focus, but I am a professional and stick to my guns about honouring commitments and doing what I say I will do.

4. Based on the above description, a commitment to how you will manage your workload in this programme. This should include - an explanation of how you will manage yourself to work productively and safely with other learners, facilitators and industry/community representatives.

I have begun to use a 30min pomodoro timer to keep track and to work on focused 30min sprints, and then to have a break to keep me fresh, and then to focus on another 30min sprint. I will be exercising in the mornings to keep me fresh and energised. I will be professional in my actions, with respecting and honoring those around me. I have a keen interest in networking and will be respectful of other peoples time and their commitments to their study.

5. A commitment as to how and where you will seek help in a timely way.

If I come across a roadblock, I will set my pomodoro timer for 1 hour, and do my best to figure it out. If not I will turn to Stack overflow, and google to figure it out, if I haven't after 30 minutes I will reach out to my cohort and those on slack.

6. A description of what you expect from the facilitation team

I want someone to help point me in the right direction to work out the solutions needed, rather than being 'shown to the well' so to speak. Also, sometimes I can delve to deep in an area of interest but which is a tanget to what I should be focusing on. I guess to some extent I enjoy keeping myself accountable and checking in on what I am doing. So perhaps 2 video zoom session a week would be great during Foundations.
